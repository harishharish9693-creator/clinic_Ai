Neuroanatomy

Neuroanatomy is the study of the structure and organization of the nervous system, including the brain, spinal cord, and nerves. It explains how different parts of the nervous system are connected and how they control body functions.

Neurogenetics

Neurogenetics is the branch of science that studies how genes influence the development, function, and disorders of the nervous system. It combines neurology (study of the nervous system) and genetics (study of heredity).

Learn More

cellular and molecular neuroscience

Cellular and molecular neuroscience is the branch of neuroscience that studies the nervous system at the level of cells (neurons and glial cells) and molecules (proteins, DNA, RNA, neurotransmitters). It explains how these microscopic components work together to control brain function and behavior.
